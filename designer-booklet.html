I created a 12-page booklet on an admired contemporary designer, for which I chose Kate Moross. The front and back cover of the booklet uses a single sheet of transparent acetate and the center spread uses a single sheet of vellum. The rest of the pages, in addition to the sleeve, are printed on pearlescent paper and the project is bound with a wax string using the pamphlet stitch. Almost all of Moross's works featured in the booklet were acquired through their online portfolio. The center spread was created by editing and image tracing a mural painted in Tokyo while the featured products were illustrated on Adobe Photoshop by me. The illustration on the front of the booklet sleeve was also my addition to the booklet in order to create a cohesive theme. This project was assigned in Introduction to Graphic Design during RISD 2020 Wintersession.
